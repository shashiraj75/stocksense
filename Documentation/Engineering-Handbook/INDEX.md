Companion: [Valuation Intelligence Research Report](Architecture/Valuation-Intelligence-Research-Report.md). **Verdict: further design work required (an India feasibility study, mirroring Epic 003's own precedent) before full-scope coding begins; US-side implementation of the highest-confidence metric subset can proceed in parallel.**

- [Sprint #004 — Valuation Intelligence Calibration & Production Validation](Releases/Sprint-004-Valuation-Intelligence-Calibration-Production-Validation.md) — validates the v1 engine against **406 real companies (254 India, 152 US)**, explicitly tagged by category (bank, NBFC, premium compounder, value/low-P/E, cyclical, REIT/real estate, utilities, capital-intensive, distressed, growth) per this sprint's own brief. **Central finding: a real, structural false-positive pattern, not a threshold bug** — financially distressed companies with degenerate, extreme multiples (e.g. `RELINFRA` P/E 1.33 → score 100; `RELCAPITAL` P/E 0.58 → score 83) score near the engine's maximum, confirmed via live data to be mathematically correct given the inputs, not an arithmetic defect. Root cause: Valuation Intelligence by design (SSDS-008's own Evidence Checkpoint) does not assess earnings quality or solvency — that gap belongs to Business Quality and Financial Strength, never duplicated here. **No threshold was changed** — tightening any band would not fix the conceptual gap and would be exactly the speculative tuning this sprint's rules forbid; the finding is named as a load-bearing constraint for any future Prediction Engine integration (must combine with Financial Strength's solvency signal), not "fixed" by a number change. Premium compounders confirmed correctly classified as expensive against real, verified multiples (NESTLEIND P/E 79, DMART P/E 95.6) — **not** unfairly penalized. Banks/NBFC population-gating reconfirmed correct on a larger, more adversarial sample. No genuine code defects found; no new tests added per the sprint's own "regression coverage only for genuine defects" rule; 695/695 unchanged. **Recommendation: Ready for Outcome Validation**, carrying forward the standalone-consumption caveat above.
- [Sprint #005 — Valuation Intelligence Outcome Validation](Releases/Sprint-005-Valuation-Intelligence-Outcome-Validation.md) — measures real forward-return signal against **81 India + 54 US companies**, using a single-window price-rescaling proxy methodology (mirroring Growth Intelligence's own Sprint #005 precedent, adapted for a price-ratio-based engine — reconstructs an approximate "score as of N months ago" by rescaling price-dependent fields by real historical price ratios, holding fundamentals fixed). **Forward-return correlation is weak-to-negative at 3-6 months, strengthening to moderate-positive at 12 months in both markets** (Spearman ρ India +0.272, US +0.418) — a real, horizon-dependent signal, not overfit. **Ranking buckets are monotonic in both markets**: cheapest decile outperformed most-expensive decile by ~20pp (India) and ~53pp (US) at 12 months. **The decisive finding**: `RELINFRA` scored 73/100 (buy-adjacent) 12 months before realizing a **-82.0% return** — direct, quantitative confirmation of Sprint #004's standalone-consumption caveat, not just architectural reasoning. Distressed is India's single worst-performing category by realized return (-19.6%) despite a moderate-to-high average score. **Cross-engine analysis (read-only, no engine modified) found Growth Intelligence's existing score independently flagged 3 of the 4 worst value-trap candidates as "avoid"** using unrelated data — real evidence that combining engines mitigates most, not all, of the false positives (the exception, `BPCL`, fails for regulatory-pricing reasons neither engine measures). REITs diverge sharply by market (India's low score correctly anticipated a real decline; US's equally low score missed a real AI-data-center-driven rally) — a named, evidence-consistent regime effect, not engine inconsistency. No thresholds changed, no engines modified. **Recommendation: Ready for Integration Readiness Decision.**
- [Sprint #006 — Valuation Intelligence Integration Readiness Decision](Releases/Sprint-006-Valuation-Intelligence-Integration-Readiness-Decision.md) — a decision sprint, no code touched. **Confirms Sprint #004's value-trap constraint remains valid**, now the single best-evidenced risk caveat of any engine in this codebase (`RELINFRA` 73/100 → real -82.0% return). **Unlike Growth Intelligence's own India-only precedent, this decision applies to both markets uniformly** — since the value-trap risk is structural to the engine's design, not market-specific, evidence from both markets shows real positive 12-month correlation (India ρ +0.272, US ρ +0.418). **Selects Option C: Confidence-only with strict safeguards.** Designs (does not implement) an asymmetric cap — **+2 boost (gated) / -4 demotion (ungated)** — directly operationalizing the brief's Standalone Consumption Rule: undervaluation may only boost confidence when at least one quality/resilience engine (India: Business Quality OR Growth Intelligence; US: + Financial Strength) does not itself grade AVOID/REJECTED, drawing directly on Sprint #005's own finding that Growth Intelligence independently flagged 3 of 4 worst value traps; overvaluation may demote confidence unconditionally, since warning about a verified-expensive stock carries none of the boost side's promotion risk. Per-market kill switches (`VALUATION_INTELLIGENCE_CONFIDENCE_ENABLED_IN`/`_US`), defaulting to **disabled** — a more conservative default than Growth Intelligence's own rollout, given this engine's uniquely severe demonstrated downside. Ranking/Daily Picks filtering withheld in both markets. **Recommendation: Ready for Prediction Engine Integration** (Sprint #007), scoped to implement exactly this safeguard design, not a generic confidence-only wiring.

Double-counting reviewed for all three named pairs (Growth vs. PEG, Earnings Growth vs. Forward P/E, Cash Flow Growth vs. FCF Yield) — no material overlap found; Growth Intelligence has no "Cash Flow Growth" category at all, stated explicitly rather than assumed. - [Sprint #003 — Growth Intelligence Engine v1 Implementation](Releases/Sprint-003-Growth-Intelligence-Engine-v1.md) — implements exactly the v1 metric set SSDS-007/the Feasibility Study scoped: `growth_intelligence_engine.py` (7 categories, EngineResponse contract), `india_growth_adapter.py`, `us_growth_adapter.py`, plus two narrow additive changes to `screener_data.py` (extracting the existing `eps_trend` logic into a shared `growth_utils.py` utility both adapters reuse, and preserving the multi-year margin series the scraper already parsed but previously discarded). Live-validated against **85 India + 61 US real companies** (146 total, zero crashes) — every directional sanity check held (high-growth/turnaround scored highest, declining lowest, in both markets independently), with extreme scores spot-checked against real, known events (TSLA's documented 2023-24 margin compression, specialty chemicals' down-cycle, GE/DIS's real turnarounds) rather than assumed correct. Banks/NBFCs gracefully degrade to ~43% confidence, never rejected. **Two genuine defects found and fixed during the sprint's own test-writing** (an acceleration-bonus dead-code bug, and a CAGR crash on a negative terminal value) — both now covered by dedicated regression tests. 50 new tests, 586/586 full suite passing. No consumer integration, no Prediction Engine changes, Business Quality/Financial Strength confirmed untouched. **Recommendation: not yet ready for Prediction Engine integration** — needs one more, smaller calibration sprint first.
- [Sprint #004 — Growth Intelligence Calibration & Production Validation](Releases/Sprint-004-Growth-Intelligence-Calibration.md) — expanded live validation to **123 India + 117 US real companies** (246 total, exceeding the 100-company minimum), covering every named segment (compounders, hyper-growth, mature, cyclicals, turnarounds, decliners, banks, NBFCs, IT, Pharma, FMCG, Industrials, Utilities, Energy). **Zero scoring-level false positives or false negatives found** — every extreme score spot-checked traced to genuine, real underlying metrics. **One genuine explainability defect found and fixed**: a marginal ±3 EPS Trend signal was qualifying as the headline (sometimes only) "strength" for companies the engine itself scored 6/100 "avoid" (COALINDIA, SRF) — fixed with a new presentation-layer filter (`MIN_NOTABLE_CONTRIBUTION`), not a scoring-weight change. Confirmed a real corporate-action distortion risk in Reinvestment Efficiency (RELIANCE's bonus-issue jump falls directly inside its 3Y lookback window) and one unconfirmed scraper gap (BANDHANBNK) — both named for a future sprint, not fixed speculatively. Performance confirmed negligible (sub-millisecond engine/adapter cost; the ~1.5s yfinance fetch is the pre-existing, already-shared cost other engines amortize). 3 new regression tests, 589/589 passing. No Prediction Engine integration, Business Quality/Financial Strength confirmed untouched. **Recommendation: needs another calibration sprint** — specifically, validation against forward outcomes (not yet performed for this brand-new engine), not further sanity-checking.
- [Sprint #005 — Growth Intelligence Outcome Validation](Releases/Sprint-005-Growth-Intelligence-Outcome-Validation.md) — the forward-outcome validation Sprint #004 called for. Truncated each India company's multi-year arrays by one year to get a genuine "score as of ~1 year ago," then used real historical price data to compute actual forward 3/6/12-month returns — **India: a positive, monotonic correlation across all three windows** (Spearman ρ +0.15 to +0.17; top decile averaged +13.5%/12mo vs. bottom decile's -6.4%), plus a strong Stability Review result (ρ=0.751 between 1-year-ago and current scores). **Found and reported a real methodological limitation**: the identical truncation approach failed for US, since yfinance's ~4-5yr depth leaves no headroom once a year is dropped (111/112 "truncated" US scores were artificial REJECTEDs, not genuine signal) — pivoted to anchor US scores to their own latest fiscal date instead, which **revealed a negative correlation** (ρ=-0.437) in the only well-powered US window. Direct false-signal investigation found an identical, consistent cross-market pattern: every false positive was a genuine high-growth name caught in an apparent growth-to-value rotation; every false negative was a genuine low-growth cyclical/commodity name that rebounded — attributed primarily to a valuation/macro regime effect, not a measurement defect, but **not conclusively ruled out as persistent** given the single-window limitation. No Prediction Engine integration, no scoring-weight changes. **Recommendation: Minor refinement required** — specifically, re-validate the US correlation once enough time has passed for non-clustered anchor windows, before integration.
- [Sprint #006 — Growth Intelligence Integration Readiness Decision](Releases/Sprint-006-Growth-Intelligence-Integration-Readiness-Decision.md) — a decision sprint synthesizing Sprints #001-#005's evidence; no code, scoring, or consumer-integration change made. **Final decision: Integrate India-only confidence signal** — India's evidence (positive monotonic correlation, strong ρ=0.751 rank stability, zero scoring defects) clears the same bar Financial Strength cleared before its own integration; US is withheld for confidence (not just "insufficient evidence" but an *adverse* result in the only well-powered window) while still receiving full explainability output in both markets regardless of the numeric gate. Confidence-adjustment cap recommended at **±3** (smaller than Financial Strength's ±6, proportionate to Growth Intelligence's more modest correlation magnitude) with a hard market gate, a kill switch, and continuous (not one-time) outcome monitoring as required risk controls. Ranking and Daily Picks filtering withheld for **both** markets — one calibration cycle's evidence doesn't yet clear the higher bar a harder-to-reverse commitment than a soft confidence nudge deserves. Names the valuation/macro rotation risk explicitly as a permanent characteristic of growth-factor signals (not a defect), with eventual pairing against a future Valuation Intelligence engine as the long-term mitigant. Defines the specific evidence threshold required before US confidence integration is reconsidered (non-clustered anchor windows, non-negative correlation at n≥80, a completed US Stability Review).
- [Sprint #007 — Growth Intelligence Prediction Engine Integration (India Only)](Releases/Sprint-007-Growth-Intelligence-Prediction-Engine-Integration.md) — implements exactly Sprint #006's approved design. Wires `_get_growth_intelligence` into `prediction_engine.py`'s existing Round-2 `asyncio.gather` (computed for **both** markets, for explainability/telemetry) and `_apply_growth_intelligence_adjustment` into the existing confidence chain, after Financial Strength's own adjustment. New, independent kill switch (`GROWTH_INTELLIGENCE_CONFIDENCE_ENABLED_IN`/`_US`, env-var-backed, defaults enabled/disabled respectively, fail-safe on a malformed value) plus the hard `market == "IN"` check provide defense-in-depth — confirmed by dedicated tests that either control alone suffices. New `±3` cap (`GROWTH_INTELLIGENCE.PREDICTION_ENGINE_CONFIDENCE_ADJUSTMENT_CAP`, distinct from Financial Strength's own `±6`). Validated against **155 real India + 117 real US companies** (exceeding the required 150/100 minimums) run through the actual new adjustment function — zero crashes, every India adjustment within ±3 with a real non-degenerate distribution, every US adjustment exactly 0. Confirmed empirically negligible added latency (India's screener.in fetch hits an existing 4-hour cache, 0.002ms on the call this integration actually makes; US reuses the already-shared ticker, zero new network calls). 51 new tests (integration/regression/kill-switch/graceful-degradation/golden-explainability), 635/635 full suite passing. `growth_intelligence_engine.py` itself, Business Quality, and Financial Strength all confirmed byte-for-byte unchanged. **Recommendation: Ready for Daily Picks Validation.**
- [Sprint #008 — Growth Intelligence Daily Picks Validation](Releases/Sprint-008-Growth-Intelligence-Daily-Picks-Validation.md) — confirms Daily Picks consumes Growth Intelligence exactly as intended, against **209 real India + 130 real US companies** (exceeding the required 200/120 minimums). **Decisive finding, confirmed empirically not just by reading the source**: `ranking_alpha` (Daily Picks' actual Top-6 sort key, computed by `_zscore_and_rank()` purely from tech/fund/sentiment/quality z-scores) was bit-for-bit identical whether or not Growth Intelligence's confidence adjustment was applied, across every one of 339 real-company evaluations in both markets — confirming Growth Intelligence has zero path into ranking. The one real, intended effect found: a strong/weak Growth Intelligence score can rescue or sink an already-borderline (near the 25%-confidence noise floor) stock's Daily Picks *eligibility*, never its rank order — 8 of 209 India companies flipped when confidence was deliberately sampled near that boundary, zero flipped when sampled from a realistic general range; every flip traced to a real, correctly-attributed Growth Intelligence score. Confirmed no string-collision between Growth Intelligence's reasoning entries and the pre-existing quality gate's `"Risk/Reward"`/`"Governance Risk"`/`"liquidity distress"`/`"Overbought"` exclusion checks. No genuine defect found — 10 new regression tests, 645/645 full suite passing, no production scoring/ranking/engine code modified. **Recommendation: Ready for Epic 003 Closure.**

## How to use this handbook

1. Before starting any implementation task, read SES-001–005 if you haven't already internalized them this session.
2. Check the [Product Glossary](Glossary/StockSense360-Product-Glossary.md) before naming any engine, score, or feature — use the name it specifies, don't invent a new one.
3. For anything spanning more than one engine or service, read [SSDS-000](SSDS/SSDS-000-StockSense360-System-Architecture.md) first. Before starting a feature or a sprint, fill out the matching SSDS-001/002 template.
4. While working, follow the relevant SES standard for the kind of change you're making.
5. When you finish a sprint, write its report under `Releases/` per SES-004 §3, and update this INDEX if you added a new governing document.
