## System Design Specifications (SSDS)

- [SSDS-000 — StockSense360 System Architecture](SSDS/SSDS-000-StockSense360-System-Architecture.md) — the master architecture document; the single source of truth for how every component of the platform fits together. Read this before designing anything that spans more than one engine or service.
- [SSDS-001 — Feature Specification Template](SSDS/SSDS-001-Feature-Specification-Template.md) — for any user-facing feature or material behavior change.
- [SSDS-002 — Sprint Specification Template](SSDS/SSDS-002-Sprint-Specification-Template.md) — for scoping a numbered engineering sprint against the Roadmap.
- [SSDS-003 — StockSense360 Business Quality Engine](SSDS/SSDS-003-StockSense360-Business-Quality-Engine.md) — full design spec (framework, metrics, sector adaptation, output contract, validation strategy) for the engine answering "is this fundamentally an outstanding business worthy of long-term ownership?" Implemented in Sprint #004 (`backend/services/business_quality_engine.py`), validated and calibrated across Sprints #004a, and integrated into its first consumer (Multibagger Quality Compounder, US-only) in [Sprint #005](Releases/Sprint-005-Business-Quality-Engine-Multibagger-Integration.md).
- [SSDS-004 — StockSense360 India Fundamentals Data Strategy](SSDS/SSDS-004-StockSense360-India-Fundamentals-Data-Strategy.md) — strategy proposal identifying two unverified data hypotheses as the highest-leverage next step before any provider decision. **Both hypotheses verified — see the validation study below.**
- [SSDS-005 — StockSense360 Financial Strength Intelligence Engine](SSDS/SSDS-005-StockSense360-Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Engine.md) — full design spec (scope boundary vs. Business Quality Intelligence, scoring categories, Financial Stress Simulation framework, data requirements, sector adaptations, confidence model, output contract, validation strategy, future sprint roadmap) for the engine answering "could this company survive a downturn and service its obligations?" Formalizes the [Financial Strength Intelligence Design Study](Architecture/Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Design-Study.md) into a binding specification. **Specification only — no code implemented.** Next sprint: a Sprint #006-style data-feasibility study, per market, before implementation begins.
- [India Fundamentals Data Validation & Derivation Study](Architecture/India-Fundamentals-Data-Validation-Study.md) — 65-company live validation of SSDS-004's hypotheses. **Result: the existing screener.in pipeline already supports 4 of 5 targeted Business Quality Engine metrics (Altman, Sloan, Cash Conversion, Asset Turnover) at 97–100% completeness** — the Total-Assets-via-balance-sheet-identity hypothesis is proven (97% cross-check match against an independent source, both outliers individually explained). Only Beneish M-Score is a confirmed, total gap (0/65). **Recommends no new data provider and Sprint #007 implement a narrowly-scoped India Business Quality Adapter** (the 4 proven metrics only) — not further research, not an NSE/paid-vendor build.
- [SSDS-006 — StockSense360 Data Fabric & Provider Architecture](SSDS/SSDS-006-StockSense360-Data-Fabric-Provider-Architecture.md) — the permanent, provider-agnostic foundation every future data source must be built against: a 7-layer pipeline (Provider Adapter → Normalization → Validation → Confidence → Data Fabric resolution → Cache → Engines) with a standing Provider Adapter Standard, a field-normalization schema, per-field provenance tagging, a provider-confidence model kept explicitly separate from engine-level confidence, failover rules, and a multi-provider conflict-resolution policy. Formalizes ideas the Data Independence & Provider Strategy report only proposed informally into a binding spec. **Specification only — no code implemented, no existing provider or engine touched.** Recommends building the SEC EDGAR Adapter first (Section 15), since it is the lowest-risk, highest-value, most evidence-backed next step and generates the real multi-provider data every later Fabric layer needs to be calibrated against.

## Epics

- [EPIC-001 — Business Quality Intelligence (CLOSED)](EPICS/EPIC-001-Business-Quality-Intelligence-Closure.md) — the permanent closure record for the Business Quality Engine effort (Sprints #003–#007). Read this single document, not every sprint report below, to understand what was built, why, how it was validated, and what remains intentionally out of scope (Beneish M-Score, the Altman financial-sector-exemption refinement, switching on the Multibagger scorecard's IN consumption). Recommends proceeding to Epic 002 — Financial Strength Intelligence.
- **Epic 002 — Financial Strength Intelligence (specification complete; data-feasibility study complete with material gaps found; first provider implemented):** [SSDS-005 — StockSense360 Financial Strength Intelligence Engine](SSDS/SSDS-005-StockSense360-Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Engine.md) is the frozen specification — scope boundary vs. Business Quality Intelligence, five scoring categories, the Financial Stress Simulation framework, data requirements, sector-adaptation hypotheses, confidence model, `EngineResponse` mapping, and the validation/sprint roadmap. Formalizes the [Financial Strength Intelligence — Design Study](Architecture/Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Design-Study.md) (the original precursor document; still useful for its narrative reasoning). [Sprint #001 — SSDS-005 Data Validation Report](Architecture/SSDS-005-Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Data-Validation-Report.md) ran the commissioned data-feasibility study live against 70 US companies (full sample, strong coverage) and 15 of 74 targeted India companies (the rest blocked mid-study by screener.in's unauthenticated-access IP-block — named as an operational risk, not glossed over). **Result: Conditional Go for US; No-Go for India as scoped today** — Liquidity Adequacy and the debt-maturity-split dimension of Leverage & Capital Structure have no confirmed data path for India, and both markets' FINANCIAL sector (banks/NBFC/insurance) needs a sector-specific substitute for universal liquidity/leverage metrics. No code implemented; recommends a credentialed re-run plus four scoped follow-ups before implementation begins. [Sprint #002 — Data Independence & Provider Strategy](Architecture/Epic-002-Sprint-002-StockSense360-Data-Independence-Provider-Strategy.md) is a platform-wide data-strategy study (not Financial Strength-specific) answering whether StockSense360 can become independent of Screener.in. **Finding: partially** — confirmed live that SEC EDGAR (free, official, zero current integration, 17-year history) can become the new primary US fundamentals source, while India has no free structured equivalent and remains credential-gated on Screener.in for the 12–24 month horizon. Recommends building a SEC EDGAR adapter for US and restoring authenticated Screener.in access for India as the two next, lowest-risk actions. No code implemented. [Sprint #003 — SSDS-006 Data Fabric & Provider Architecture](SSDS/SSDS-006-StockSense360-Data-Fabric-Provider-Architecture.md) formalizes the permanent multi-provider architecture (see SSDS section above) every future provider, including the recommended SEC EDGAR Adapter, must be built against. No code implemented. [Sprint #004 — SEC EDGAR Adapter](Releases/Sprint-Epic002-004-SEC-EDGAR-Adapter.md) implements `backend/services/sec_edgar_adapter.py` — the first provider built under SSDS-006's Provider Adapter Standard: CIK resolution, rate-limit-safe HTTP access, field normalization, and full per-field provenance for every SSDS-005-required US fundamentals field. Confirmed live for 5 representative tickers (AAPL, MSFT, JPM, KO, ORCL) — correctly surfaced JPM's real, FINANCIAL-sector absence of current-asset/liability/EBIT tags (independently cross-confirming SSDS-006's own finding) and a new gap (`total_liabilities` missing for KO/ORCL) not previously known from yfinance-only testing. **296/296 tests passing** (262 pre-existing + 34 new), zero regressions to the existing yfinance path. Purely additive — no existing provider, engine, or India path touched; not yet wired into any engine or consumer. [Sprint #005 — SEC EDGAR Large-Scale Coverage Validation](Architecture/Epic-002-Sprint-005-SEC-EDGAR-Large-Scale-Validation.md) re-runs the adapter live against the same 70-company SSDS-005 universe plus 6 REITs (76 total, zero fetch errors), comparing every SSDS-005-required field against yfinance. **Found and fixed two genuine adapter defects during validation** (a stale/deprecated XBRL tag winning over current data — confirmed for MSFT/TFC/CAT/AMZN; a quarterly fact masquerading as annual inside a 10-K) — `net_income` rose to 100% coverage, `total_assets`/`operating_cash_flow`/`shareholders_equity` confirmed at 100% with near-zero disagreement. Confirmed, at scale, that the FINANCIAL-sector liquidity gap is real and structural (5.3% EDGAR / 0.0% yfinance across 19 banks+insurance companies) and newly found that REITs share much of that same structural gap. Named, unfixed gaps: lease liabilities missing from debt tags, `total_liabilities` derivable-but-not-derived for 15 companies. **311/311 tests passing.** Recommends EDGAR as primary for `total_assets`/`net_income`/`operating_cash_flow`/`shareholders_equity` specifically, not yet as a blanket replacement; provider precedence unchanged, EDGAR still not wired into any engine. [Sprint #006 — US Provider Precedence Decision](Architecture/Epic-002-Sprint-006-US-Provider-Precedence-Decision.md) decides field-level (not provider-level) US precedence for all 16 SSDS-005-required fields from Sprint #005's evidence: EDGAR primary for revenue/net_income/current_assets/current_liabilities/total_assets/operating_cash_flow/capital_expenditure/shareholders_equity/interest_expense (non-FINANCIAL); yfinance primary for ebit/total_liabilities/short_term_debt/long_term_debt/total_debt/free_cash_flow/interest_expense (FINANCIAL sector). Two fields (`cash_and_equivalents`, `ebit`) are named as open *definitional* decisions, not resolved by precedence. Confirms FINANCIAL-sector and (newly) REIT-sector substitute requirements. Implements one small, additive module, `backend/services/us_provider_precedence.py` (field-level resolution + fallback-on-absence, never on-disagreement), justified by Sprint #005's evidence per this sprint's own "implement only if evidence supports it" rule — **not imported by any engine, provider, or consumer**. **350/350 tests passing** (311 prior + 39 new). No engine, recommendation logic, or India provider modified. [Sprint #007 — Financial Strength Data Readiness Re-Check](Architecture/Epic-002-Sprint-007-Financial-Strength-Data-Readiness-Recheck.md) re-runs the precedence module against Sprint #005's real 76-company dataset: **average applicable-field completeness is 98.3% (FINANCIAL 96.2%, REIT 96.1%, other sectors 99.4%) — zero companies fall below SSDS-005's own 60% confidence threshold.** US readiness raised to **8/10** (substantiated, not just hoped-for); India readiness **unchanged at 4/10** — no India-side work occurred in the intervening sprints, named honestly rather than implied as "in progress." New finding: `capital_expenditure`'s residual gap is sector-concentrated (8/10 FINANCIAL, 2/10 REIT) exactly like the existing sector-substitute fields — flagged as a Sprint #008 candidate, not added this sprint (no genuine defect found, so provider precedence is unchanged per this sprint's own rule). Recommends proceeding **US-first**, explicitly mirroring Epic 001's Sprint #005 precedent, while India remains a named, untouched gap. No code changed; 350/350 tests confirmed unchanged. [Sprint #008 — Financial Strength Intelligence Engine v1](Releases/Sprint-Epic002-008-Financial-Strength-Engine-v1.md) **implements the engine**: `backend/services/financial_strength_engine.py` (pure scoring, zero provider I/O, zero Business Quality metric duplication) + `backend/services/us_financial_strength_adapter.py` (EDGAR + yfinance + precedence resolution). Scope: non-FINANCIAL, non-REAL_ESTATE (REIT-proxy) US companies only. Live-validated against the full 76-company universe: 25 correctly excluded (19 FINANCIAL, 6 REAL_ESTATE), 49/51 eligible companies scored (0 insufficient-data, 2 individually-explained `liquidity_distress` gate triggers — AAL, AEP), 99.4% average data completeness, a sane non-degenerate grade distribution. **Found and fixed two genuine defects during its own required validation**: numpy scalar types breaking JSON serialization, and REITs (PLD, PSA) slipping past the v1 exclusion due to a sector-classifier keyword collision (`industrial` matching before `REAL_ESTATE`'s patterns) — fixed locally in the new adapter, deliberately not in the shared Business-Quality-owned classifier, to avoid silently changing BQE's own shipped scores. **394/394 tests passing** (350 prior + 44 new). Not wired into `PredictionEngine` or any consumer — recommends a dedicated, separately-scoped integration sprint next, mirroring Business Quality's own Sprint #005 precedent. [Sprint #009 — Production Validation & Calibration](Releases/Sprint-Epic002-009-Financial-Strength-Engine-Validation-Calibration.md) reviews Sprint #008's real per-company results against investor expectations and **finds and fixes two genuine defects**: a sign-inversion bug where negative shareholders' equity (real LUMN data) produced a debt-to-equity ratio the engine misread as a "strength"; and a `liquidity_distress` hard-gate false positive for AEP, confirmed systematic by comparing against DUK/SO/NEE's near-identical structural liquidity profile (utilities normally run a low current ratio — AEP was rejected and DUK wasn't purely because AEP's ratio fell fractionally below the same 0.5x cutoff DUK's fell fractionally above). AAL's own hard-gate trigger was reviewed and judged justified, unmodified — its profile is independently corroborated outside the engine's own output, unlike AEP's. Fixed via a new `LIQUIDITY_GATE_EXEMPT_SECTOR_BUCKETS = {"UTILITIES_ENERGY"}` exemption (hard gate only — the underlying soft-score under-scoring for this sector is named, not recalibrated, pending more sector evidence) and a guard against computing debt-to-equity when equity is non-positive. **401/401 tests passing** (394 prior + 7 new). Recommends proceeding to a dedicated Sprint #010 first-consumer integration next. No Prediction Engine, Daily Picks, recommendation logic, or India provider modified. [Sprint #010 — Controlled Integration into Prediction Engine](Releases/Sprint-Epic002-010-Financial-Strength-Prediction-Engine-Integration.md) makes `PredictionEngine` the engine's first consumer (US equities only) via a new, bounded `_apply_financial_strength_adjustment()` — confidence-only, never `composite_score`/`signal`, mirroring the existing `_apply_risk_reward_adjustment`/`_apply_pledge_adjustment` extension point rather than wiring into `_composite_signal`'s weighting (which would be exactly the Prediction Engine redesign this sprint forbids). Controlled validation across the real 76-company universe (228 comparisons at 3 baseline confidence levels) confirmed every named recommendation-quality question with real evidence: fortress companies (MSFT, GOOGL) moved up to the +6 cap; the most leveraged company (BA) moved down; AEP's post-Sprint-#009 calibration produces a correct soft demotion (no longer hard-gated); AAL's hard gate still correctly caps confidence at 30. Zero false positives found; two named, deliberate false negatives (DUK/SO's sector-structural under-scoring) traced directly to Sprint #009's already-documented, intentionally-deferred gap. **431/431 tests passing** (401 prior + 30 new). Recommends a narrow Daily Picks *validation* pass next (not a new build, since Daily Picks already consumes Prediction Engine's output automatically); Portfolio Copilot doesn't exist yet to integrate into; Recommendation Consolidation is explicitly sequenced after Growth/Valuation/Risk Intelligence per the Master Roadmap.

## How to use this handbook

1. Before starting any implementation task, read SES-001–005 if you haven't already internalized them this session.
2. Check the [Product Glossary](Glossary/StockSense360-Product-Glossary.md) before naming any engine, score, or feature — use the name it specifies, don't invent a new one.
3. For anything spanning more than one engine or service, read [SSDS-000](SSDS/SSDS-000-StockSense360-System-Architecture.md) first. Before starting a feature or a sprint, fill out the matching SSDS-001/002 template.
4. While working, follow the relevant SES standard for the kind of change you're making.
5. When you finish a sprint, write its report under `Releases/` per SES-004 §3, and update this INDEX if you added a new governing document.
