## System Design Specifications (SSDS)

- [SSDS-000 — StockSense360 System Architecture](SSDS/SSDS-000-StockSense360-System-Architecture.md) — the master architecture document; the single source of truth for how every component of the platform fits together. Read this before designing anything that spans more than one engine or service.
- [SSDS-001 — Feature Specification Template](SSDS/SSDS-001-Feature-Specification-Template.md) — for any user-facing feature or material behavior change.
- [SSDS-002 — Sprint Specification Template](SSDS/SSDS-002-Sprint-Specification-Template.md) — for scoping a numbered engineering sprint against the Roadmap.
- [SSDS-003 — StockSense360 Business Quality Engine](SSDS/SSDS-003-StockSense360-Business-Quality-Engine.md) — full design spec (framework, metrics, sector adaptation, output contract, validation strategy) for the engine answering "is this fundamentally an outstanding business worthy of long-term ownership?" Implemented in Sprint #004 (`backend/services/business_quality_engine.py`), validated and calibrated across Sprints #004a, and integrated into its first consumer (Multibagger Quality Compounder, US-only) in [Sprint #005](Releases/Sprint-005-Business-Quality-Engine-Multibagger-Integration.md).
- [SSDS-004 — StockSense360 India Fundamentals Data Strategy](SSDS/SSDS-004-StockSense360-India-Fundamentals-Data-Strategy.md) — strategy proposal identifying two unverified data hypotheses as the highest-leverage next step before any provider decision. **Both hypotheses verified — see the validation study below.**
- [SSDS-005 — StockSense360 Financial Strength Intelligence Engine](SSDS/SSDS-005-StockSense360-Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Engine.md) — full design spec (scope boundary vs. Business Quality Intelligence, scoring categories, Financial Stress Simulation framework, data requirements, sector adaptations, confidence model, output contract, validation strategy, future sprint roadmap) for the engine answering "could this company survive a downturn and service its obligations?" Formalizes the [Financial Strength Intelligence Design Study](Architecture/Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Design-Study.md) into a binding specification. **Specification only — no code implemented.** Next sprint: a Sprint #006-style data-feasibility study, per market, before implementation begins.
- [India Fundamentals Data Validation & Derivation Study](Architecture/India-Fundamentals-Data-Validation-Study.md) — 65-company live validation of SSDS-004's hypotheses. **Result: the existing screener.in pipeline already supports 4 of 5 targeted Business Quality Engine metrics (Altman, Sloan, Cash Conversion, Asset Turnover) at 97–100% completeness** — the Total-Assets-via-balance-sheet-identity hypothesis is proven (97% cross-check match against an independent source, both outliers individually explained). Only Beneish M-Score is a confirmed, total gap (0/65). **Recommends no new data provider and Sprint #007 implement a narrowly-scoped India Business Quality Adapter** (the 4 proven metrics only) — not further research, not an NSE/paid-vendor build.
- [SSDS-006 — StockSense360 Data Fabric & Provider Architecture](SSDS/SSDS-006-StockSense360-Data-Fabric-Provider-Architecture.md) — the permanent, provider-agnostic foundation every future data source must be built against: a 7-layer pipeline (Provider Adapter → Normalization → Validation → Confidence → Data Fabric resolution → Cache → Engines) with a standing Provider Adapter Standard, a field-normalization schema, per-field provenance tagging, a provider-confidence model kept explicitly separate from engine-level confidence, failover rules, and a multi-provider conflict-resolution policy. Formalizes ideas the Data Independence & Provider Strategy report only proposed informally into a binding spec. **Specification only — no code implemented, no existing provider or engine touched.** Recommends building the SEC EDGAR Adapter first (Section 15), since it is the lowest-risk, highest-value, most evidence-backed next step and generates the real multi-provider data every later Fabric layer needs to be calibrated against.

## Epics

- [EPIC-001 — Business Quality Intelligence (CLOSED)](EPICS/EPIC-001-Business-Quality-Intelligence-Closure.md) — the permanent closure record for the Business Quality Engine effort (Sprints #003–#007). Read this single document, not every sprint report below, to understand what was built, why, how it was validated, and what remains intentionally out of scope (Beneish M-Score, the Altman financial-sector-exemption refinement, switching on the Multibagger scorecard's IN consumption). Recommends proceeding to Epic 002 — Financial Strength Intelligence.
- **Epic 002 — Financial Strength Intelligence (specification complete; data-feasibility study complete with material gaps found; first provider implemented):** [SSDS-005 — StockSense360 Financial Strength Intelligence Engine](SSDS/SSDS-005-StockSense360-Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Engine.md) is the frozen specification — scope boundary vs. Business Quality Intelligence, five scoring categories, the Financial Stress Simulation framework, data requirements, sector-adaptation hypotheses, confidence model, `EngineResponse` mapping, and the validation/sprint roadmap. Formalizes the [Financial Strength Intelligence — Design Study](Architecture/Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Design-Study.md) (the original precursor document; still useful for its narrative reasoning). [Sprint #001 — SSDS-005 Data Validation Report](Architecture/SSDS-005-Financial-Strength-Intelligence-Data-Validation-Report.md) ran the commissioned data-feasibility study live against 70 US companies (full sample, strong coverage) and 15 of 74 targeted India companies (the rest blocked mid-study by screener.in's unauthenticated-access IP-block — named as an operational risk, not glossed over). **Result: Conditional Go for US; No-Go for India as scoped today** — Liquidity Adequacy and the debt-maturity-split dimension of Leverage & Capital Structure have no confirmed data path for India, and both markets' FINANCIAL sector (banks/NBFC/insurance) needs a sector-specific substitute for universal liquidity/leverage metrics. No code implemented; recommends a credentialed re-run plus four scoped follow-ups before implementation begins. [Sprint #002 — Data Independence & Provider Strategy](Architecture/Epic-002-Sprint-002-StockSense360-Data-Independence-Provider-Strategy.md) is a platform-wide data-strategy study (not Financial Strength-specific) answering whether StockSense360 can become independent of Screener.in. **Finding: partially** — confirmed live that SEC EDGAR (free, official, zero current integration, 17-year history) can become the new primary US fundamentals source, while India has no free structured equivalent and remains credential-gated on Screener.in for the 12–24 month horizon. Recommends building a SEC EDGAR adapter for US and restoring authenticated Screener.in access for India as the two next, lowest-risk actions. No code implemented. [Sprint #003 — SSDS-006 Data Fabric & Provider Architecture](SSDS/SSDS-006-StockSense360-Data-Fabric-Provider-Architecture.md) formalizes the permanent multi-provider architecture (see SSDS section above) every future provider, including the recommended SEC EDGAR Adapter, must be built against. No code implemented. [Sprint #004 — SEC EDGAR Adapter](Releases/Sprint-Epic002-004-SEC-EDGAR-Adapter.md) implements `backend/services/sec_edgar_adapter.py` — the first provider built under SSDS-006's Provider Adapter Standard: CIK resolution, rate-limit-safe HTTP access, field normalization, and full per-field provenance for every SSDS-005-required US fundamentals field. Confirmed live for 5 representative tickers (AAPL, MSFT, JPM, KO, ORCL) — correctly surfaced JPM's real, FINANCIAL-sector absence of current-asset/liability/EBIT tags (independently cross-confirming SSDS-006's own finding) and a new gap (`total_liabilities` missing for KO/ORCL) not previously known from yfinance-only testing. **296/296 tests passing** (262 pre-existing + 34 new), zero regressions to the existing yfinance path. Purely additive — no existing provider, engine, or India path touched; not yet wired into any engine or consumer.

## Engineering Audits

- [SEAR-001 — Selection Engine Engineering Audit](Architecture/Sprint-001-Selection-Engine-Audit.md) — the founding audit; source of the Roadmap's phases and most named risks/gaps referenced throughout the standards above.
- [Business Quality Engine — Production Readiness Validation](Architecture/Business-Quality-Engine-Production-Readiness-Validation.md) — the founding 55-company live validation. Found Altman Z-Score/Sloan Accruals never computed against live data and Piotroski had no sector-awareness — both fixed in [Sprint-004a](Releases/Sprint-004a-Business-Quality-Engine-Calibration.md). That fix surfaced a follow-up false positive (HON/ORCL hard-rejected) — root-caused (Altman's X1/X2/X3 numerator terms had no fallback, only the denominator did) and fixed, commit `ffaafcb`.
- [Business Quality Engine — Final Production-Readiness Re-Validation](Architecture/Business-Quality-Engine-Final-Production-Readiness-Revalidation.md) — full Phase 1–9 re-validation after all three fixes, on a fresh live run of the same 53-company dataset. **Verdict: production-ready for the scope validated.** Altman/Accruals availability 100%, HON/ORCL false rejections confirmed fixed, genuine distress (IDEA/LCID/RIVN/PTON) still correctly rejected, no new false positives/negatives.
- [Sprint #005 — Business Quality Engine → Multibagger Integration](Releases/Sprint-005-Business-Quality-Engine-Multibagger-Integration.md) — the recommended first consumer, integrated. **US-only** by evidence-based necessity (the IN nightly refresh sources from screener.in and has no yfinance `Ticker` in scope; adding one would be a broad refactor out of scope) — named explicitly, not silently scoped down. Promotion-only and red-flag-only additions to the scorecard; both pre-existing golden tests pass unchanged. 194/194 tests passing, GitHub Actions green.
- [Sprint #007 — India Business Quality Adapter](Releases/Sprint-007-India-Business-Quality-Adapter.md) — closes Sprint #005's IN gap. A new `india_business_quality_adapter.py` maps screener.in data into the engine's input model (using Sprint #006's proven Total-Assets-via-identity derivation) and is wired into the IN nightly refresh — populating Business Quality scores for India through the unmodified engine, no new provider. **Result on the full 65-company validated universe: 0 errors, 0 rejections, Altman/Sloan/Cash-Conversion all 100%, stable 91.7% confidence.** A genuine classifier defect was found and fixed along the way (screener.in's real Indian sector labels — "Fast Moving Consumer Goods", "Packaged Foods", "Power - Transmission" — fell through to `OTHER`). 262/262 tests passing. The Business Quality Engine now populates scores for **both** markets; next consumer step is wiring the IN fields into the Multibagger scorecard (already market-agnostic since Sprint #005).

## How to use this handbook

1. Before starting any implementation task, read SES-001–005 if you haven't already internalized them this session.
2. Check the [Product Glossary](Glossary/StockSense360-Product-Glossary.md) before naming any engine, score, or feature — use the name it specifies, don't invent a new one.
3. For anything spanning more than one engine or service, read [SSDS-000](SSDS/SSDS-000-StockSense360-System-Architecture.md) first. Before starting a feature or a sprint, fill out the matching SSDS-001/002 template.
4. While working, follow the relevant SES standard for the kind of change you're making.
5. When you finish a sprint, write its report under `Releases/` per SES-004 §3, and update this INDEX if you added a new governing document.
